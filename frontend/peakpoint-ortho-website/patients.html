Be Prepared

New Patient Checklist

Bring these items to your first appointment to help us provide the best possible care.

Required Items

  • Photo ID – Driver's license, passport, or state ID
  • Insurance card – Front and back (if applicable)
  • Completed intake forms – Download below or arrive 15 min early

Helpful to Have

Your Appointment

What to Expect

Your first visit typically takes 30-45 minutes. Here's what will happen during your evaluation:

1
Medical History ReviewWe'll discuss your symptoms, when they started, and what you've already tried.
2
Physical ExaminationA focused exam to assess your range of motion, strength, and areas of concern.
3
Imaging ReviewIf you brought prior imaging, we'll review it together. We may recommend additional studies.
4
Treatment Plan DiscussionClear explanation of your diagnosis and recommended next steps (therapy, injections, follow-up, etc.).

We do not provide diagnosis or medication instructions through the website or over the phone. All treatment decisions happen after an in-person evaluation.

Important

When to Seek Urgent Care

Some symptoms require immediate medical attention. If you experience any of the following, seek urgent care or go to the emergency room.

Possible Fracture or Dislocation

Visible deformity, bone/joint visibly out of place, or severe pain and swelling immediately after trauma.

Can't Bear Weight or Use Limb

Complete inability to stand, walk, or use your arm/leg after an injury—not just pain with movement.

Neurologic Red Flags

New weakness, progressive numbness, or severe back pain with bowel/bladder changes (loss of control).

Go to the ER if:

You have severe, worsening symptoms, open wounds with bone visible, signs of infection (fever, hot swollen joint, red streaking), or any life-threatening emergency.

Call us for urgent (non-emergency) needs:

If your situation is urgent but stable—like a possible sprain or acute injury where you can still function—call (555) 907-1148. We often have same-day appointments available.
