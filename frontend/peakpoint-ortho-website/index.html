FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about our services, appointments, and what to expect during your visit.

Still Have Questions? Contact Us
Do you offer same-day appointments?

Yes, we often have same-day appointments available for urgent injuries. Call us at (555) 907-1148 as early as possible to check availability. We prioritize acute injuries that need immediate evaluation.

Can you review imaging (X-rays/MRIs) I already have?

Absolutely. You can email your imaging to appointments@peakpointortho.example or bring a disc to your appointment. Please call to confirm we received your images before your visit. This helps us prepare and make the most of your appointment time.

What should I bring to my first appointment?

Please bring:

• Photo ID and insurance card (if applicable)
• List of current medications and allergies
• Any prior imaging reports or discs
• Brief timeline of your symptoms (when it started, what makes it better/worse)

Do you provide medical advice online or over the phone?

No. While we can share general information and help route you to appropriate care, diagnosis and specific treatment recommendations require an in-person evaluation with a clinician. This ensures we can properly assess your condition and provide safe, accurate guidance.

What insurance plans do you accept?

We accept most major insurance plans. For specific questions about your coverage, please call our office at (555) 907-1148. Our team can verify your benefits and explain any out-of-pocket costs before your appointment.

How long does a typical appointment take?

Initial evaluations typically take 30-45 minutes, depending on the complexity of your condition. Follow-up appointments are usually 15-20 minutes. We recommend arriving 15 minutes early for your first visit to complete any necessary paperwork.

Do you treat sports injuries for all ages?

Yes! We treat athletes of all ages and skill levels—from youth sports to recreational adult leagues to competitive athletes. Our sports medicine team understands the unique needs of different age groups and activity levels.

When should I go to the ER instead of calling your office?

Seek emergency care if you experience:

• Visible bone deformity or bone protruding through skin
• Inability to bear weight after an injury
• Severe swelling that develops rapidly
• New weakness, numbness, or loss of function
• Fever with a hot, swollen joint
• Severe back pain with bowel/bladder changes

Do you offer physical therapy on-site?

We coordinate closely with trusted physical therapy partners and can provide referrals based on your location and needs. While we don't offer PT on-site, we ensure seamless communication between our office and your therapy team for optimal recovery.

What happens if I need surgery?

If surgery is recommended, we'll discuss all your options, explain the procedure in detail, and coordinate the entire process. This includes pre-surgical planning, the procedure itself, and post-operative rehabilitation. We'll be with you every step of the way.
