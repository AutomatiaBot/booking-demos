Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about orthodontic treatment at AlignBright.

View All FAQs
What's the difference between aligners and braces?

Clear aligners are removable, nearly invisible trays that gradually shift teeth—perfect for adults and teens who want discretion. Braces use fixed brackets and wires for precise control, ideal for complex cases. Both are highly effective, and we'll help you choose the best option for your lifestyle and goals.

How long does orthodontic treatment typically take?

Treatment duration varies based on your unique case. Minor corrections may take 6-12 months, while moderate cases average 12-18 months. Complex cases can take 18-24+ months. During your free consultation, we'll provide a personalized timeline based on your specific needs.

What if I lose or break my retainer?

Contact us immediately—we understand these things happen! We can create a replacement retainer within days to protect your investment. Delaying can lead to teeth shifting, so prompt action is important. We keep digital records on file for quick replacements.

Am I too old for orthodontic treatment?

Absolutely not! We treat patients of all ages, and adult orthodontics is more popular than ever. Clear aligners are especially popular with adults due to their discretion. Whether you're 25 or 65, a healthy, beautiful smile is achievable.

Does orthodontic treatment hurt?

Most patients experience mild discomfort for a few days after adjustments or new aligner trays—this is normal and means treatment is working! Over-the-counter pain relievers help, and any discomfort typically fades quickly. Modern braces and aligners are more comfortable than ever.

What should I expect at my first appointment?

Your first visit includes a comprehensive exam, digital X-rays and photos, and a detailed discussion of your goals. We'll explain all your options, answer every question, and provide a clear treatment plan with transparent pricing—no pressure, just information to help you decide.
