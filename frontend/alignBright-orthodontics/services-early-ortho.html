Early Orthodontics

Home / Services / Early Orthodontics

Start Early for Best Results

The American Association of Orthodontists recommends children have their first orthodontic evaluation by age 7. Early intervention can guide jaw growth and prevent more complex treatment later.

Schedule an Evaluation

Why Age 7?

By age 7, enough permanent teeth have emerged to identify potential issues with jaw growth, tooth crowding, or bite alignment. Early evaluation doesn't always mean early treatment—but it does mean we can monitor development and intervene at the optimal time.

Signs Your Child May Need Evaluation

  • Early or late loss of baby teeth
  • Difficulty chewing or biting
  • Mouth breathing or snoring
  • Thumb sucking past age 5
  • Crowded or misplaced teeth
  • Jaws that shift or make sounds

Benefits of Early Treatment

Guide jaw growth
Create space for emerging teeth
Reduce risk of trauma to protruding teeth
Simplify or shorten future treatment
Correct harmful oral habits

What to Expect

During an early evaluation, we'll examine your child's teeth and jaws, take any necessary images, and discuss our findings. If treatment is recommended, we'll explain the options. Many children simply need monitoring until more teeth emerge.
