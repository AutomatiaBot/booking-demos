Getting Started

What should I expect at my first visit?

Your first visit includes a thorough examination, digital scans or X-rays, and a discussion of your goals. We'll explain your treatment options, estimated timeline, and costs. The visit typically takes 45-60 minutes.

How do I know if I need orthodontic treatment?

Signs you may benefit from treatment include crooked or crowded teeth, gaps, bite problems (overbite, underbite, crossbite), jaw pain, or difficulty chewing. Schedule a consultation for a professional evaluation.

How long does treatment usually take?

Treatment length varies by case. Simple alignment may take 6-12 months; more complex cases can take 18-24 months or longer. We'll provide a personalized estimate during your consultation.

How often are check-in appointments?

For braces, typically every 4-8 weeks. For aligners, check-ins are usually every 6-10 weeks. We'll establish a schedule based on your specific treatment plan.

Clear Aligners

What's the difference between aligners and braces?

Clear aligners are removable, nearly invisible trays that gradually shift teeth. Braces use fixed brackets and wires for precise control. Both are effective—the best choice depends on your specific case, lifestyle, and preferences.

Do aligners work for teens?

Yes! We offer aligner programs designed specifically for teens, with compliance indicators and coaching support. Success depends on wearing aligners 20-22 hours daily. We'll help determine if aligners are right for your teen.

How often do I need to wear my aligners?

Aligners should be worn 20-22 hours per day for best results. Remove them only for eating, drinking anything other than water, and brushing your teeth.

How do I clean my aligners?

Rinse aligners with lukewarm water each time you remove them. Clean them daily with a soft toothbrush and clear, mild soap. Avoid hot water, which can warp the plastic.

Braces

What are ceramic braces?

Ceramic braces work the same as metal braces but use tooth-colored or clear brackets that blend with your teeth. They're a popular choice for adults and teens who want a less noticeable option.

Can I play sports or musical instruments with braces?

Yes! We recommend wearing a mouthguard during sports to protect your braces and teeth. Most patients adjust to playing instruments within a few weeks. We can provide tips for specific instruments.

What foods should I avoid with braces?

Avoid hard foods (nuts, hard candy, ice), sticky foods (caramel, gum), and chewy foods (bagels, tough meat). Cut hard fruits and vegetables into small pieces. Your orthodontist will provide a complete list.

Retainers

What if I lose or break my retainer?

Contact us immediately at (562) 555-0117. Teeth can begin shifting within days without a retainer. We can create a replacement quickly to protect your results.

How long do I need to wear my retainer?

Most patients wear retainers full-time initially (except eating and brushing), then transition to nighttime wear. Many orthodontists recommend wearing retainers at night indefinitely to maintain results.

How do I clean my retainer?

Clean your retainer daily with a soft toothbrush and mild soap or retainer cleaner. Rinse thoroughly. For fixed retainers, use a floss threader to clean around the wire.

Kids & Teens

When should my child have an orthodontic evaluation?

The American Association of Orthodontists recommends an evaluation by age 7. This allows us to identify potential issues early and plan for optimal treatment timing.

Does my child need treatment if they still have baby teeth?

Not always. Early evaluations help us monitor development. Some children benefit from early intervention to guide jaw growth; others simply need monitoring until more permanent teeth emerge.

Comfort & Urgent Issues

Is it normal for teeth to be sore after adjustments?

Yes, mild soreness for 2-4 days after adjustments or new aligner trays is normal. Over-the-counter pain relievers and soft foods can help. If pain is severe or persists, contact us.

What should I do if a bracket comes loose or wire is poking?

For a poking wire, try gently pushing it with a pencil eraser or covering it with orthodontic wax. For loose brackets, call us to schedule a repair. Don't try to remove brackets yourself.

Payment & Insurance

Do you accept insurance?

Yes, we accept most major dental insurance plans with orthodontic benefits. Our team will verify your coverage and help you understand your benefits.

Do you offer payment plans?

Yes, we offer flexible payment plans to make treatment affordable. We can discuss options during your consultation, including monthly payment arrangements and financing options.
